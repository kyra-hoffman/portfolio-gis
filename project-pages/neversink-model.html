Neversink Reservoir Model
New York City has a system of 19 reservoirs to provide drinking water to its citizens. As part of a research project investigating the impact of the reservoir construction on the local communities, I developed a 3D model of one area and the buildings that were standing before it was submerged under water.
Project Details / Background
In the summer of 2022, I was a research fellow at Vassar College’s Undergraduate Research Summer Institute. I was part of a project that was investigating the connection between the creation of the New York City reservoir system and the culture of destruction in the communities around the waterbodies. My part of the project was to map buildings that were removed or destroyed as part of reservoir creation. To better visualize the flooding of a valley, I created a 3D model of the Neversink Reservoir in Neversink, NY. I used NYS DEM data and NYC DEC reservoir bathymetry data to create the model of the landscape and the water level. The buildings present are from data collected from Sanborn Insurance maps and field work to the site.
