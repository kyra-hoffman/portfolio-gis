Vassar College Sustainability Map
The sustainability map for Vassar College acts as a point of interaction for many campus stakeholders with the Office of Sustainability. The map is an important hub of information that prospective and current students can learn about renewable energy and waste reduction.
Project Details / Background
As part of my position as the Sustainability Education Fellow at Vassar College, I pushed to use my familiarity with GIS to update and improve the College’s sustainability map. The previous map was created by a past student for their senior thesis and had become outdated. It was a missed opportunity for storing and sharing institutional knowledge about sustainability initiatives. This map’s update coincided with a revamping of the Office of Sustainability website.
Using the existing map as a jumping off point, I transferred the map from ArcGIS webmaps to ArcGIS Web Builder. I organized data points into sustainability categories (landscape, transportation, waste, energy, engagement, academics, and facilities). However, this brings up the issue of information belonging in multiple categories. To address this issue, the symbology coloring and description shows any category relation.
