Undergrad Thesis: Suitability Model
My senior thesis topic was about investigating the presence of beavers and how they interact with water quality and humans around them. It became a case study of the Casperkill stream in Poughkeepsie, NY. A culmination of my research was the creation of a suitability model for beaver presence along the Casperkill stream.>
Project Details / Background
One portion of my undergraduate thesis was developing a suitability model for beaver presence on the Casperkill stream that goes through the Vassar College Campus using a weighted sum analysis with multiple criteria. This model was informed by both human-beaver and beaver-environment interactions. There were 5 criteria: vegetation, slope, distance from roads, land use, and width of riparian barrier. I further investigated the ownership of land within a set buffer zone, private vs. public to understand different management strategies.
The analysis highlighted 3 most suitable locations. One of the locations, the Vassar Preserve, had confirmed beaver presence. One location was above an industrial mining site which shows that this model should be improved upon to include industrial sites such as mining and logging. The last site was not investigated but was all private property and so governmental management interventions were not possible. The conclusion from my work is that more work needs to be done to create a connective corridor for wildlife in this watershed.
